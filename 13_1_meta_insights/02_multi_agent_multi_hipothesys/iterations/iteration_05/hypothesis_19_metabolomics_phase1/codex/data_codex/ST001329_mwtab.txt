#STUDY
ST:STUDY_TITLE                   	SS-31 and NMN: Two Paths to Improve Metabolism and Function in Aged Hearts
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	The effects of two different mitochondrial-targeted drugs, SS-31 and NMN, were
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	tested on Old mouse hearts. After treatment with the drugs, individually or
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	Combined, heart function was examined by echocardiography. SS-31 partially
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	reversed an age-related decline in diastolic function while NMN fully reversed
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	an age-related deficiency in systolic function at a higher workload. Metabolomic
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	analysis revealed that both NMN and the Combined treatment increased
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	nicotinamide and 1-methylnicotinamide levels, indicating greater NAD+ turnover,
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	but only the Combined treatment resulted in significantly greater steady state
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	NAD(H) levels. A novel magnetic resonance approach was used to assess how
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	metabolite levels responded to changing workload. PCr/ATP decreased in response
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	to increased workload in Old Control, but not Young, hearts, indicating an
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	age-related decline in energetic capacity. Both drugs were able to normalize the
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	PCr/ATP dynamics. SS-31 and NMN treatment also increased mitochondrial NAD(P)H
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	production under the higher workload while only NMN increased NAD+ in response
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	to the work jump. These measures did not shift in hearts given the Combined
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	treatment, which may be owed to the enhanced NAD(H) levels in the resting state
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	after this treatment. Overall, these results indicate that both drugs are
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	effective at restoring different aspects of mitochondrial and heart health and
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	that combining them results in a synergistic effect that rejuvenates Old hearts
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	and best recapitulates the Young state.
